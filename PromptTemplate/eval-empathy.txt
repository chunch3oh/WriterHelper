Evaluation Criteria:
- Relatability: How well the characters and their experiences resonate with the reader.
- Emotional Engagement: The extent to which the story evokes emotions in the reader.
- Perspective-Taking: The story's ability to encourage readers to see the world from the characters' perspectives.
- Character Development: How the characters evolve throughout the story as a result of their experiences.

Evaluation Steps:
1. Read the story carefully and identify the main characters and their roles.
2. Analyze character development, assess relatability, and evaluate emotional engagement.
3. Examine perspective-taking and consider the narrative style.
4. Reflect on your own emotional response to the story.
5. Assign a score for each evaluation criterion (Relatability, Emotional Engagement, Perspective-Taking, and Character Development) on a scale of 1 to 10, with 1 being the lowest and 10 being the highest.
